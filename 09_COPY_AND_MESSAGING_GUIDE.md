# Copy and Messaging Guide

## Core copy principle

RideRational copy should create emotional possibility without sounding inflated. The user should feel intrigued, relieved, and impressed—not pitched at.

## Homepage copy framework

### Message stack
1. emotional hook
2. product truth
3. proof of contrast
4. how it works
5. trial CTA

### Recommended hero structure
- eyebrow: sweet-spot used cars, found locally
- headline: contrast-driven
- subhead: explain the product clearly
- CTA: action-oriented and low-friction

### Example hero copy
**Eyebrow:** Premium and performance sweet spots, found locally  
**Headline:** For what you were about to spend anyway, you may be able to drive something far more special.  
**Subhead:** RideRational finds used premium and performance cars that are already past the worst depreciation, then emails the strongest local opportunities daily.  
**CTA:** Start with your taste

## Teaser value copy

### Objective
Prove possibility without replacing the paid product.

### Rules
- do not present it as full access
- do not reveal live shortlist depth
- do not sound like a fake reveal
- do not imply exact certainty where only category logic exists

### Example teaser headline
Buyers like you may be overlooking something much more interesting.

### Example teaser narrative
Instead of spending that comfort-zone budget on the obvious default, you may be able to target a used sweet spot that delivers a very different ownership experience.

## Lightweight setup microcopy

### Tone
- short
- reassuring
- helpful
- not robotic

### Example lines
- Tell us where your comfort zone really is.
- We use this to filter out the wrong kind of temptation.
- You can update these preferences anytime.
- Daily emails only work if we understand your taste clearly.

## Trust copy

### Trust themes
- the product does the hunting
- we are not asking you to browse endlessly
- we are not pretending the teaser is the full product
- we focus on categories where desirability and economics intersect

### Example trust strip lines
- Not another endless listing rabbit hole.
- Not a generic affordability quiz.
- Not a broad marketplace.
- A daily shortlist, tuned to your taste and comfort zone.

## CTA copy

### Best primary CTAs
- Start with your taste
- See what may be possible
- Start your free trial
- Set my preferences
- Get daily sweet-spot picks

### Secondary CTAs
- See how it works
- Review the setup
- Manage preferences

## Objection handling copy

### Objection: “I can browse listings myself.”
Response pattern:
You can. RideRational exists so you do not have to sort through the noise every day.

### Objection: “Is this just a calculator?”
Response pattern:
No. The economics matter, but the outcome is still a desirable car you actually want to drive.

### Objection: “Will this just show cheap cars?”
Response pattern:
No. The product is tuned for sweet spots, not bargain-bin randoms.

### Objection: “Why pay for this?”
Response pattern:
The value is not one teaser screen. It is the ongoing daily curation, filtering, and explanation.

## Safe language

Use:
- may
- could
- often
- likely
- comfort zone
- sweet spot
- benchmark/default
- more special
- stronger match
- local opportunity

## Prohibited language

Do not use:
- guaranteed savings
- best deal in the market
- secret hack
- beat the system
- insider advantage
- AI-powered miracle
- luxury for pennies
- alpha
- winning the market

## Premium public-facing translation of strong conversion energy

Internal conversion intent may be aggressive. Public copy should translate that into:
- composure
- specificity
- sharp contrast
- credible restraint

### Instead of
“Shut up and take my money.”

### Public-facing equivalent
“This feels sharp, useful, desirable, and easy enough that I’m comfortable starting the trial.”

## Copy rules for possibility without giving too much away

1. show contrast, not full inventory depth
2. explain logic, not every field
3. use local opportunity counts sparingly
4. blur or lock deeper result modules
5. make the daily email the promised unlock
6. never let the teaser feel like the complete product
