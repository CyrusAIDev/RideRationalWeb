# Email Product Spec

## MVP email definition

The daily email is the core product delivery surface in MVP.

Its job is to help the subscriber quickly understand:
- what looks interesting today
- why it matches their preferences
- why it may be a sweet spot
- why it may be more compelling than the obvious default

## Email cadence

### Default cadence
- daily

### Optional preference variants
- weekdays only
- paused

## Recommended email composition

### Total deals per email
- **1 hero deal**
- **2 to 3 supporting deals**

This keeps the email sharp and curated rather than list-like.

## Hero deal structure

The hero deal should include:

- vehicle name
- year / trim
- asking price
- estimated monthly TCO range or anchor
- location
- mileage
- listing source
- primary image
- why this matches you
- why this is a sweet spot
- benchmark/default contrast line
- CTA to view listing or manage preferences

## Supporting deal card structure

Each supporting deal should include:
- vehicle name
- price
- location
- short sweet-spot note
- short match note
- CTA to view listing

## “Why this matches you” structure

Use 1 to 2 short bullets or one concise sentence covering:
- chosen category
- budget fit or comfort-zone fit
- lifestyle/taste alignment
- transmission/body style alignment if relevant

Example:
Matches your performance-sedan preference and sits inside the monthly comfort zone you set for a usable daily driver with more character than the default choice.

## “Why this is a sweet spot” structure

Use 1 concise sentence covering:
- depreciation logic
- desirability logic
- comparative logic

Example:
Already past the steepest depreciation window, but still highly desirable in this category—making it more interesting than the obvious default at similar spend.

## Benchmark/default comparison structure

Every hero deal should connect back to a benchmark/default concept.

### Example format
Instead of defaulting to a newer mainstream compact SUV at this spend, this sweet spot gives you a much more distinctive ownership experience in roughly the same broad comfort zone.

## Subject line rules

### Subject line goals
- specific
- premium
- curiosity-led
- not spammy

### Good patterns
- Today’s RideRational picks for your performance-sedan brief
- A stronger sweet spot just surfaced in Vancouver
- You may want to see today’s top luxury SUV match
- Today’s more-interesting option at your comfort zone

### Avoid
- ALL CAPS
- urgency spam
- fake scarcity
- “save thousands now”
- overlong titles

## Preview text rules

Preview text should:
- support the subject line
- reference match quality or comparison logic
- stay calm and credible

Example:
A more special local option than the obvious default—plus two supporting matches.

## Trust language rules

The email should feel:
- curated
- calm
- informed
- useful

It should not feel:
- like a coupon blast
- like an inventory dump
- like dealership marketing
- like a hype newsletter

## Preference and unsubscribe behavior

Every email must include:
- manage preferences
- pause emails
- unsubscribe

These should feel respectful and easy to find.

## MVP value delivery rule

The daily email is not a reminder about the product. It **is the product** in MVP.
